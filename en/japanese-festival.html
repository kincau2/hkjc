Japanese Festival: The Ultimate Cultural Celebration at Happy Wednesday Returns!

The crowd’s favourite Japanese Festival is back at Happy Valley Racecourse this November, presented by The Hong Kong Jockey Club in partnership with the Consulate-General of Japan in Hong Kong and the Japan Racing Association. Experience a dazzling fusion of racing excitement and cultural immersion, including the Japan Racing Association Trophy on 5 November, alongside themed entertainment, cultural experience and authentic Japanese flavours. Mark your calendars: the festivities kick off with the day race on 2 November (Sun), followed by the night races on 5, 12 and 19 November.

Feel the Energy in the Beer Garden
International singer Natalia D will take centre stage at the Beer Garden, delivering high-energy pop hits, joined by our lively dancers. Get ready to sing, dance, and celebrate!

Enter the Lucky Draw* for a Chance to Win a Designated Valued HK$8,000 Travel Voucher to Japan (4 Winners Total)
On the four Happy Wednesday Japanese Festival race days, customers with a net spending of HK$100 or more at the designated Beer Garden food trucks at Happy Valley Racecourse can register on-site to enter the lucky draw*. Simply follow the official Instagram page of HKJC Entertainment (@HKJCEntertainment) and publish a photo taken at the Happy Wednesday Japanese Festival on Instagram (as a post or story), tagging @HKJCEntertainment. This gives you a chance to win the grand prize of a designated valued HK$8,000 travel voucher to Japan*, with one grand prize winner per event day.

Engage with Japanese Culture in a Yukata
Step into a stunning Japanese set-up in the racecourse, decorated with vibrant red maple leaves, glowing lanterns, and iconic torii gates, all set for your photos. Try on a Japanese Yukata for a fun photo session with friends at the themed photo booth1!

Savour the Flavours of Japan
Get ready for a culinary adventure that will transport you straight to Tokyo! Delight in authentic Japanese dishes like Crispy Shrimp Cake, Hiroshima Yaki, Green Tea Pudding with Red Bean Paste and Sakura Latte Waffle from the Beer Garden food trucks and stalls, paired with iced beer, Japanese sake or sake cocktails. On 5 November, a special sake truck will be set up, offering free tasting of a designated sake served in traditional wooden masu cups2.
